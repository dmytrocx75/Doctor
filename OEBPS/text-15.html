The next afternoon was full of patients with a new strain of ﬂu.
George spent his time going from one exam room to the next,
talking to his patients, and remembering why he loved being a
doctor.
When he ﬁnally had a break in his appointments, George decided
to take a walk to clear his head and think a little more about
the medical center and the fate of his practice. As he walked,
his mind wandered. “What if one of my patients can’t get in for
an appointment with me and switches to a new doctor at the
medical center?” he thought. “What if the center’s slick equipment
is a draw? And they probably accept all kinds of insurance.”
George felt light-headed and stumbled to a bench. “I’ve got to
pull myself together,” he chided himself. “I hate this business
stuff, but I have to do it. No way am I going to let this medical
center scuttle my life’s work.” George Osborne would not give up
without a ﬁght.

Contemplation

While waiting for Wendy to arrive for their strategy session,
George studied the sticky notes from the brainstorming session
and wondered how they translate into a plan to save his business.
His staff had generated some great ideas. George was glad to
have some options now.
“Of course, I can get new furniture and hire an IT guy. But what

about opening a home health service? Wendy’s right. If I want
to be competitive, I need to focus on long-term projects ﬁrst.
But, which one?” George suddenly felt overwhelmed. He needed
Wendy’s advice.
When they met the next day, Wendy asked George, “Now that you
have a list of opportunities, any thoughts on where to start?”
