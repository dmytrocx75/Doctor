“George, give me a minute to explain. Go/No Go is ﬁne when you
have only a few options and the answer is pretty straightforward. The
problem is when you have many factors to consider and they all seem
important. Weighting each factor gives you a way to formalize your
thought process and diﬀerentiate between choices when the answer
is not clear. Another advantage to weighting is that it helps you avoid
making a decision based on your biases.”
Wendy continued, “So, George, what are the success factors that you
feel are most critical in your solution?”
George thought for a minute and oﬀered, “Certainly, cost-effectiveness
is important. And the amount of time it will take to implement. First and
foremost, the solution must not disturb my work with patients. Also, if I
invest in something, I want it to beneﬁt as many patients as possible.”
“Great! Let’s list them and assign the weights from 1 to 10, with 10
being the highest.”
George plopped down at his desk. “Okay, Wendy, now what?”
“The only thing left is to multiply the weights by the ratings for each
